Why Keep a Decision Journal?

The Problem

Most people evaluate decisions by their outcomes. But outcomes are influenced by luck. A good decision can have a bad outcome, and a bad decision can have a good outcome. Without a journal, you can't tell the difference.

Hindsight bias rewrites our memories. After we know the outcome, we "remember" being more or less confident than we actually were. A decision journal captures your actual state of mind at the time of the decision.

The Solution

By recording your reasoning BEFORE you know the outcome, you create an honest record that you can review later. Over time, you'll discover patterns in your thinking, identify systematic errors, and calibrate your confidence.

Research shows that people who keep decision journals improve their prediction accuracy by 20-30% within 6 months. Start with KeepRule's decision principles as your foundation.
